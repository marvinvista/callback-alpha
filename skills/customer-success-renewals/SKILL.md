# Customer Success Renewals

## Overview

Use this skill to move a customer from signed deal to realized value and then to renewal.

The goal is to make outcomes visible early enough that renewal is a continuation of success, not a surprise negotiation.

## When to Use

- Use when a new customer needs an implementation plan.
- Use when the team needs a success plan, QBR, renewal prep, or expansion logic.
- Use when retention risk needs to be made concrete.

## When Not to Use

- Do not use for first-touch selling or prospecting.
- Do not use when the user only wants a one-off support reply.
- Do not use when no customer outcome has been defined yet; define success first.

## Inputs

- Customer account context
- Expected use case and desired outcomes
- Contract term or renewal timing
- Known risks, adoption data, or stakeholder notes

## Workflow

1. Define the customer’s target outcomes and the timeframe for realizing them.
2. Create a lightweight implementation plan with owners, milestones, and dependencies.
3. Decide what adoption and value signals will show that the account is healthy.
4. Schedule the communication rhythm:
   - onboarding touchpoints
   - milestone reviews
   - business reviews
   - renewal checkpoints
5. Surface risks early:
   - low adoption
   - unclear owner
   - poor outcome definition
   - unresolved product gaps
6. Prepare renewal strategy well before the renewal window.
7. Note expansion opportunities only when the value case is real.

## Output Contract

Return:

- `Success outcomes`
- `Implementation plan`
- `Health signals`
- `Communication rhythm`
- `Risks and mitigations`
- `Renewal plan`
- `Expansion signals`, if justified

## Quality Bar

- Do not confuse activity with customer value.
- Make renewal risk visible before the final month.
- Keep the plan tied to outcomes the customer actually cares about.
- Avoid forced expansion suggestions when the base value case is weak.
