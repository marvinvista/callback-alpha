# Narrative Framework

## Core structure

Work through these prompts in order:

1. What painful business problem exists?
2. Who feels it most acutely?
3. How is it handled today?
4. Why is the current approach insufficient?
5. What changed such that a better approach now works?
6. How does the new approach work in buyer language?
7. Why is it better in measurable or observable terms?
8. What is the pricing frame?

## Problem prompts

- What expensive, slow, risky, or frustrating workflow exists today?
- What happens if the buyer does nothing?
- Is the pain hard-cost, lost-revenue, time, quality, compliance, or strategic drag?

## Buyer prompts

- Which role owns the pain day to day?
- Which role signs off on solving it?
- Which role benefits but is not the buyer?

## Current alternative prompts

- Is the real alternative no action, a spreadsheet, a service, or another product?
- What does the buyer tolerate about the current alternative?
- What does the buyer complain about but still live with?

## Proof checklist

- Metric improvement
- Time saved
- Cost avoided
- Revenue gained
- Better recall, response, conversion, retention, or quality
- Customer quote or observed workflow improvement

If proof is missing, state the best current evidence and the next proof to collect.

## Pricing framing options

- Compare against the cost of the current alternative.
- Compare against the value created or value protected.
- Compare against a budget threshold the buyer likely controls.
- Keep the pricing explanation simple enough to say aloud on a call.

## Deliverable template

### 30-second pitch

`[Buyer] teams struggle with [problem] because [current alternative weakness]. [Market shift] now makes a better approach possible. [Product] helps them [core workflow outcome], which leads to [value].`

### 2-minute pitch

- Problem
- Buyer
- Status quo
- Why now
- Product approach
- Proof
- Pricing frame

### Sales deck storyline

1. Problem and stakes
2. Who feels it
3. Why current approaches fail
4. What changed
5. Product approach
6. Proof
7. Pricing and next step
