# Outbound Cadence

## First-touch structure

1. Reason for reaching out
2. Specific relevance signal
3. Short value hypothesis
4. Clear ask for time

## Simple seven-touch cadence

1. Day 1: email plus call
2. Day 2: call
3. Day 3: call plus voicemail
4. Day 5: follow-up email
5. Day 7: call
6. Day 9: reminder email with alternate angle
7. Day 12: break-up email

Adjust frequency if the market or title requires a lighter touch.

## Common appointment-setting objections

- `Send me information`
- `No budget`
- `Call me next quarter`
- `We already use something`
- `I am not the right person`

For each objection:

- acknowledge
- tie back to relevance
- ask one clarifying question
- restate the value of a short meeting
- ask for a time

## Meeting invite checklist

- Clear title
- Who is attending
- Purpose of the meeting
- Agenda bullets
- Location or link
- Reminder note on the day of the meeting
