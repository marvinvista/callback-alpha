# Demo And Closing

## Overview

Use this skill to structure a persuasive sales call from pre-call planning through the close.

The point is not to show every feature. The point is to move a qualified buyer toward a decision.

## When to Use

- Use when planning discovery calls, demos, pricing conversations, proposal packaging, or close strategy.
- Use when the user needs help mapping a demo to buyer pains instead of walking through screens randomly.
- Use when objections are real and need a business response rather than evasion.

## When Not to Use

- Do not use for first-touch outbound messaging.
- Do not use for pipeline cleanup without active call prep.
- Do not use if the account is unqualified; fix targeting first.

## Inputs

- Account and role context
- Known pains and discovery notes
- Demo environment or feature set
- Pricing model
- Open objections, if any

## Workflow

1. Prepare the call:
   - confirm desired outcome
   - review known pain points
   - define what must be learned
2. Open the call and confirm agenda.
3. Run discovery before presenting answers.
4. Present the problem and approach in business language.
5. Demo only the parts of the product that map to the stated pains.
6. Check for resonance with a trial close.
7. Present pricing with context, not in a vacuum.
8. Ask directly for the business or for the concrete next step.
9. Handle objections one at a time using the framework in `references/call-and-close-framework.md`.
10. If the buyer is interested but not ready to sign, package proposal options and recommend one.

## Output Contract

Return:

- `Pre-call plan`
- `Discovery questions`
- `Demo flow`
- `Likely objections and responses`
- `Pricing frame`
- `Direct ask`
- `Proposal options`, if relevant
- `Post-call next steps`

## Quality Bar

- Do not skip the ask.
- Do not let the demo become a feature parade.
- Tie every important point back to a pain, stake, or proof point.
- Treat objections as active buying signals, not as personal rejection.
