# Sales Onboarding Bootcamp

## Overview

Use this skill to build a repeatable onboarding program that gets new hires productive without throwing them into live selling too early.

The goal is a structured bootcamp, not an informal pile of docs and meetings.

## When to Use

- Use when the team needs onboarding for new SDRs, AEs, or CS staff.
- Use when ramp time is too slow or new hires are flameout risks.
- Use when training is inconsistent, ad hoc, or too dependent on one manager.

## When Not to Use

- Do not use when the task is only hiring the candidate.
- Do not use when the user only wants one training memo or one script.
- Do not use when there is no product narrative or ICP yet; those should be defined first.

## Inputs

- Role being onboarded
- Ramp target
- Product and market complexity
- Current training materials, if any
- Team cadence and tooling

## Workflow

1. Define what “productive” means for the role and the expected ramp window.
2. Build pre-work:
   - readings
   - recorded calls or demos
   - product and market primers
3. Define day-one readiness:
   - tools
   - access
   - equipment
   - admin tasks
4. Build the bootcamp modules:
   - culture and operating expectations
   - market and buyer context
   - product and demo training
   - process and tools
5. Add drilling and repetitions using `references/onboarding-plan.md`.
6. Define shadowing, live-call ramp, and manager review steps.
7. Add KPI checkpoints and an ongoing coaching rhythm after bootcamp ends.

## Output Contract

Return:

- `Ramp goal`
- `Pre-work`
- `Bootcamp modules`
- `Drills and repetitions`
- `Shadowing and live-call ramp`
- `KPIs and checkpoints`
- `Ongoing coaching cadence`

## Quality Bar

- Treat onboarding as a force multiplier, not a delay tax.
- Make repetition explicit, not optional.
- Avoid throwing new hires into live work before they can explain the problem, buyer, and product with confidence.
- Keep the ramp measurable.
